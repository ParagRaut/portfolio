Working in enterprise has it’s own benefits and detriments. On one side you get exposed to a huge company wide technical architecture and good amount of business processes and on other side once you get acquainted with it, you are mostly locked to rigid/non-bleeding edge/legacy/boring/meh/same o' same o'/you name it technology stack.

Specifically for a tinkerer like me it gets booooooring real soon. Then the real struggle starts, you get stuck in a vicious circle of learning a new technology to keep up with the ever-changing world of tech and then not being able to apply it anywhere in your work (literally anywhere), and eventually loosing grip on whatever I have learned.

This pisses me off to the great extent, changing jobs could be a viable option but then to switch to a small startup which actually keeps up with new technology could be hard for someone who has worked extensively in an Enterprise. Why you must ask ? so here’s why (totally my opinion).

1. Job security in small startups
2. Comparatively low pay
3. Multiple responsibilities on an individual (ranging from dev > test > ops > incident management ugh)
4. Tighter deadlines

Yeah yeah I agree that the things which are mentioned above can also happen in a large enterprise but chances are slimmer and very relative to one's personal experience.
This might sound more like a rant but I just wanted to express whatever I’ve experienced and the post is totally opinion based. Meanwhile let me know what do you think ? Do you agree or disagree ? 

Anyway happy coding. 😇
